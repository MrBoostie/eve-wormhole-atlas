Operating doctrine

This site assumes temporary or semi-mobile wormhole operations, not anchoring a neon sign that says “please evict me.”

1 · Eyes

Scan first

Put at least two scanners in the chain. Bookmark actual wormhole objects, not probe results, and log life/mass state before moving value.

2 · Haul

Viators only

For routine logistics, use blockade runners. Split loads, cloak-MWD correctly, never send the whole expedition budget through one untrusted exit.

3 · Run

Controlled windows

PvE, gas, salvage, and hauling happen only with pickets, fresh signatures checked, and an abort call everyone understands.

4 · Leave

Extraction is a skill

Stage exit bookmarks, cache probes, safe-log critical hulls, and keep enough liquidity outside the hole to recover from bad luck or worse decisions.

Hard rule: if the map is stale, the operation is paused. If a new signature appears, treat it as hostile access until proven otherwise.

Operational details

Daily field SOP

Do these in order. If something feels optional, that is usually the part that kills you.

Opening scan

  • Check all existing bookmarks against current signatures.
  • Resolve every new sig before value moves.
  • Record wormhole type, destination, life, mass, and time.
  • Verify no K162 surprise was ignored because everyone was “pretty sure.”

Movement discipline

  • Scout both sides before each Viator trip.
  • Split expensive loads into multiple runs.
  • Do not jump into known camps unless the plan includes why this is not idiotic.
  • Keep probes on every independent character that can fit them.

PvE window

  • Pickets on active statics and high-value connections.
  • Combat ships aligned or pre-positioned with escape bookmarks.
  • Salvage only after combat pressure is understood.
  • Abort on new sig, hostile probes, unknown ships, or lost picket contact.
Remember: weapon timers do not stop wormhole jumps, but polarization does. Track direction and timestamps like your hull depends on it, because yes, astonishingly, it does.
