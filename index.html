Quick start

Use this when you are about to undock, not after the chain has already eaten the scout.

1. Seed

Put eyes inside first

Start with at least two scanning characters inside the candidate system. Separate accounts are preferred so one bad disconnect does not turn the operation into expensive archaeology.

2. Map

Build the chain before moving cargo

Bookmark both sides of every connection, log hole life and mass, update Pathfinder/Tripwire, and identify hostile activity before the Viator leaves station.

3. Operate

Run controlled windows

PvE only with pickets, fresh d-scan cadence, salvage discipline, and a pre-written abort call. The objective is repeatable extraction, not heroic wreck generation.

Hard rule: no blind logistics, no PvE without pickets, no expensive hull movement through an unscouted chain, and no pretending a quiet d-scan means safe.

Operating workflow

The daily loop: scout, classify, decide, execute, clean up, and leave a way home.

Anchor the map

Confirm home, statics, current exits, signatures, hostile structures, and known active locals. Every new sig is investigated or treated as hostile access.

Bookmark like a professional

Bookmark the actual wormhole object, not just the probe result. Record both sides, mass state, life state, destination class/security, and time observed.

Assign eyes

Put cloaked pickets on active statics and high-value connections. If a picket is bored, that is not a failure state. That is the job working.

Choose the window

Only run PvE, gas, hauling, or rolling when the chain is understood and the exit plan is current. If the chain changes, pause and rescan.

Extract and reset

Salvage after combat pressure is understood, move loot in split loads, safe-log critical alts, and leave the next session with a usable map.

Doctrine cards

Fast references for the repo’s operating bias: mobile first, information first, logistics second, structures later.

Scout / hunter

Astero-first early doctrine

  • Excellent early flexibility for scanning, hacking, tackle, and opportunistic hunting.
  • Carry Sisters probes, cloak, analyzers, scram, and drones.
  • Escalate to CovOps/Stratios/T3C only after the basics are boringly reliable.

Astero baseline fit

Logistics

Viator-first movement

  • Scout every transition before the hauler commits.
  • Use instadock, instaundock, perches, and split cargo runs.
  • Abort on chain churn, camp reports, bad hole state, or missing eyes.

Viator SOP

Income

C3 sites with controlled salvage

  • Run sites only after the chain is mapped and pickets are placed.
  • Keep salvage windows short; blue loot is not yours until it is out.
  • Pre-align, maintain d-scan rhythm, and define the warp-out call before damage starts.
Hole control

Rolling is a procedure, not a vibe

  • Track mass estimates, polarization timers, and rescue options.
  • Use scouts on both sides and do not roll away the only scanner.
  • Critical holes are useful tools and excellent ways to strand idiots.

Field checklists

Copy these into fleet MOTD, Discord pins, or whatever shrine you use to delay disaster.

Before logistics

  • Scout confirms every gate and wormhole transition.
  • Both sides of each hole are bookmarked from the object.
  • Hole life and mass are recorded.
  • Hauler has instadock, instaundock, cloak, and safe route notes.
  • Cargo is split so one loss is annoying, not apocalyptic.

Before PvE

  • Chain is current and mapper is updated.
  • Pickets are placed on active statics and likely entrances.
  • Fleet has an abort word and pre-aligned safe.
  • D-scan cadence is assigned, not assumed.
  • Salvage plan is separate from combat plan.
Success metric: you can stop mid-operation, explain where every active connection goes, who is watching it, what you will do if a new sig appears, and how the expensive ships get out.

Mechanics worth remembering

Small details that prevent large, stupid losses.

K162 timing

The far-side K162 does not always appear immediately. Lack of a new signature is not proof nobody can reach you.

Polarization

Track direction and time. Panic-bouncing the same wormhole direction inside five minutes is how ships become educational material.

Bookmarks

Bookmark the object on grid. Probe-result bookmarks can land outside jump range, which is a magnificent way to die at “zero.”

No asset safety

Wormhole structures do not protect assets like known-space structures. If it dies, the loot becomes a public argument.

Data/relic naming

Unsecured and Frontier sites are Sleeper combat/hacking sites, not casual pirate relic snacks.

System effects

Pulsar, Magnetar, Wolf-Rayet, Cataclysmic Variable, Red Giant, and Black Hole effects can rewrite what “good fit” means.
