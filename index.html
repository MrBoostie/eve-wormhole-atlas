Absolute beginner loop

  1. Probe cosmic signatures until the wormhole is 100%.
  2. Warp to the wormhole and bookmark the actual wormhole object.
  3. Jump through, hold cloak, bookmark the return hole immediately.
  4. D-scan, check system info, identify class/effect/statics.
  5. If anything feels wrong, leave while you still know where the door is.

Do not learn these by dying

  • There is no local intel in J-space unless someone talks.
  • Wormholes expire by time and by total mass.
  • Polarization blocks repeated same-direction jumps for five minutes.
  • A K162 is the far side of a connection and often means someone else opened access.
  • W-space structures have no asset safety. The loot fairy gets custody.

Route hubs

Thera and Turnur are public wormhole highways

They are not normal home holes. They are well-known route hubs with public scouting, public bookmarks, and public hunters. Use them for logistics and roaming; do not confuse convenience with safety.

Thera

Large shattered wormhole hub

Thera connects widely across New Eden and J-space. It is the famous wormhole subway station: excellent for shortcuts, staging, and sudden bad ideas from strangers.

Check current Thera exits
Turnur

Lowsec hub with mapped connections

Turnur is known-space lowsec with EVE-Scout mapped hub connections. Treat it like a knife-filled transit terminal: useful, campable, and never truly quiet.

Bookmark folder notes

Mechanics cheat sheet

The rules that actually kill people

Wormhole survival is mostly bookkeeping under stress. The spreadsheet has teeth.

Polarization

You cannot jump the same wormhole in the same direction twice within five minutes. Jump A→B, then B→A three minutes later, and A→B stays blocked until the first timer ends. Weapon timers do not stop wormhole jumps; polarization does.

Bookmark the object

Bookmark the wormhole itself on-grid, not merely the probe result. Probe-result bookmarks can land outside jump range, which is hilarious only to the person waiting to tackle you.

K162 means exit side

K162 is the generic far-side signature. It tells you less than an originating code and often means another system has a door into you. A fresh K162 in home is not ambience; it is a security event.

Mass stages

Wormholes have total mass and per-jump mass limits. Info text indicates fresh, reduced, or critical mass, but never exact remaining kilograms. Rolling is controlled violence with uncertainty baked in.

Life stages

Most holes live 16 or 24 hours. End-of-life text means less than four hours remains. If your route home is EOL, move like an adult or prepare to scan your way out.

K162 reveal behavior

The far-side K162 may not be visible immediately. A chain can look quiet until someone jumps or the connection ages into visibility. “No new sigs” is not proof nobody can arrive.

Sleeper relic/data traps

In wormholes, relic/data names like Unsecured or Frontier are Sleeper combat sites with hacking, not harmless pirate relic candy. If it does not name a pirate faction, expect combat.

No asset safety

Destroyed wormhole structures drop assets in space rather than sending them safely to stations. Logged-off piloted ships can safe-log, which is why emergency asset plans look ugly but practical.

System effects change doctrines

Pulsar, Magnetar, Wolf-Rayet, Cataclysmic Variable, Red Giant, and Black Hole effects alter tanks, damage, reps, missiles, speed, capacitor, bombs, and sigs. Check before copying a fit from elsewhere.

Drifter holes are strange

Drifter wormhole entrances are deadspaced; warping to them can land you far off the hole. Some k-space sides are guarded. Scout them as special terrain, not normal gates with prettier lighting.

Go / no-go

Fast decision tree

When comms get loud, use this. The void does not reward committee process.

Can we move logistics?

Yes only if both sides have eyes, hole life is acceptable, mass is not critical, no hostile probes/ships are active, and cargo is split. Otherwise stage, wait, or reroute through Thera/Turnur.

Can we run PvE?

Yes only with current signatures, pickets on active holes, combat probes watched, escape bookmarks prepared, and a salvage plan. New sig equals pause until resolved.

Should we roll?

Yes when the connection is dangerous, stale, or strategically useless and you have mass math, polarization tracking, rescue probes, and HIC/roller contingency. No timers, no roll.

Are we trapped?

Not yet if any pilot has probes, safe spots, patience, and enough ISK outside. Scan chains outward, use EVE-Scout Rescue if truly stranded, and stop making the situation louder.

Daily operating procedure

Run the chain like a system, not a vibes experiment

These are intentionally boring. Boring means repeatable. Repeatable means fewer lossmails with “lol” in local afterwards.

Opening scan

  1. Compare all bookmarks against current signatures.
  2. Resolve every new signature before value moves.
  3. Record type, destination, life, mass, jump limit, and timestamp.
  4. Identify K162s and likely activity before declaring the chain usable.

Bookmark naming

  1. Use direction: HOME → C3 123.
  2. Add life/mass: FRESH, EOL, CRIT, destination, and time checked.
  3. Keep naming consistent so tired humans can parse it.
  4. Keep tactical perches off-hole, not just zero bookmarks.

Picket discipline

  1. Put eyes on active statics and key k-space exits.
  2. Report jumps, probes, bubbles, and combat ships.
  3. Stay cloaked and boring unless ordered otherwise.
  4. If picket contact is lost, pause the op.

Extraction

  1. Move irreplaceable cargo first.
  2. Keep probes on multiple characters.
  3. Do not leave the only scanner outside the hole.
  4. Close with a final signature diff and bookmark cleanup.

Comms glossary

Words people will yell while you are already stressed

Short definitions for common wormhole calls.

Picket

A scout parked to watch a hole, structure, or route. The picket reports jumps, probes, hostile ships, bubbles, and other traffic. Boring job. Vital job.

Static

A recurring connection type in a wormhole system. When it dies, another of the same type respawns somewhere else in that system.

Roll / rolling

Intentionally collapsing a wormhole by pushing mass through it, usually to remove a hostile route or fish for a better static.

Crit

Critically mass-reduced. The hole has less than about 10% mass remaining. It may strand you. Respect it.

EOL

End of life. Less than four hours before natural collapse. Fine for scouts, questionable for hauling, cursed for complacency.

Chain

The currently mapped sequence of connected systems. Chains rot constantly; timestamps matter.

Rage roll

Repeatedly rolling statics to find content. Expensive, effective, and a beautiful way to discover who can count.

Seed

A cloaked/scanning character or asset placed in a system for future access, intel, or eviction prep.
